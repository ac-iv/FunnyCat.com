Lilies

All parts of lilies are highly toxic to cats and can lead to kidney failure.

Sago Palm

Every part of the sago palm is poisonous to cats, causing vomiting, diarrhea, and even liver failure.

Tulips

The bulbs of tulips contain toxins that can cause intense gastrointestinal irritation, drooling, loss of appetite, and heart abnormalities in cats.

Azaleas

Ingesting even a few leaves of azaleas can cause oral irritation, vomiting, diarrhea, and a drop in blood pressure in cats.

Oleander

Oleander is highly toxic to cats and can cause severe vomiting, lethargy, and heart failure.

It's crucial to research and ensure that your garden plants and flowers are safe for cats. When in doubt, consult with a veterinarian or a professional botanist familiar with pet safety.
